Author Accepted Manuscript/Version of Record Data Analysis

For this project, I collected data that compares elements of author accepted manuscripts on PubMed Central and their counterparts published in the New England Journal of Medicine. This data will be used to determine to what extent the publication of an article in a subscription-based journal adds value to the author's research and writing.

I compared the differences between the Author Approved Manuscript of Gates-funded research and the Version of Record of that article published in NEJM. I found that the vast majority of edits and changes made before publication in NEJM were mostly superficial and related only to conforming the article to NEJM's style standards.

In some instances, the article's changes in NEJM may even lower the article's value or effectiveness in communication. For example, NEJM alters the titles of many articles. Though most are similar, some of the changes are quite significant. It is possible that a researcher who is searching for a title based on the AAM may have trouble locating the article's VOR. Another area where NEJM may actually lower the value of articles is in removing or heavily editing figures and tables. This only happened in rare instances, however.
